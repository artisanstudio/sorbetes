Sorbetes is the traditional variation of ice cream made in the philippines.
It is served in small scoops (quite small in comparison with American portions) on small sugar cones.
Sorbetes came from the spanish word sorbete, meaning sorbet or sherbet.
Although sorbetes came from sorbete, the Spanish word for ice cream is helado.
Rumor has it that sorbetes came from the Spanish word sorber, which means “to suck.”
